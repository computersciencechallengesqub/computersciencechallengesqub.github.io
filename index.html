Computer Science Challenges

"Ask people to make extremely advanced technology projects they have no experience with, provide no training, and then be nice to them"

Computer Science Challenges is a new, optional module for 1st year computing students at Queen's university, Belfast. It has been designed to develop initiative, resilience, problem solving and product design skills in students. It is run using an intensive coaching style which includes the use of 2 to 1 weekly meetings as well as open and frank diaries discussing work and feelings.

The module is run by Dr John Bustard, a Computer Science academic specialising in computer vision and machine learning with over 7 years experience working in the computer games industry. His experience includes work in startups and leading technology firms. He is also the director of Qlab, a skunkworks-style organisation within Queen's which supports student projects, student societies, summer internships, hackathons and other project-based activities.
