Natural Language Processing

In 2019 the leaders in the field of AI made some significant advances in applying deep learning to understanding written information. There is an enormous quantity of structured knowledge digitised in written form. If deep learning algorithms could understand and apply this information it could enable the creation of artificial general intelligence, the holy grail of artificial intelligence work.

Developing natural language processing systems includes the following steps. Each step includes a list of possible projects you could focus on for this module:

A. Gather data

i) Use sentence similarity to create lookups into a large categorised dataset that you have gathered. Use to propose alternative sentences and to infer metadata. For example from subreddits, project gutenberg texts or movie plots

ii) Use clustering and visualisation algorithms such as SKlearn clustering and T-SNE to visualise sentence signatures from different data sources and to formalise different types of sentence and how different sources of written information communicate in different ways.

B. Extract sentence structure and identify the meaning of its words

i) Using Amazon's mechanical turk, Wordnet and WikiData create a system for labelling the factual meaning of sentences in terms of their wordnet meaning and any named entities known to wikidata.

ii) Create a tool to define the structure of stories. Labelling what characters, events, relationships etc. are conveyed by sentences. Also include factors that affect the emotion of the reader, their impression of the personality and emotional state of characters, what questions they might have about unrevealed information and how they might be feeling. Use this to demonstrate how a movie synopsis can be formally labelled.

C. Turn text into structured data

i) Experiment with question answering algorithms to extract factual data from academic papers or other non-fiction sources such as wikipedia.

ii) Develop a tool to define human understandable patterns in sentences which can be used to extract structured information. For example a sentence might have adjectives and job titles describing a person. Examine existing work to explore how these human understandable rules could be automatically inferred using AI techniques applied to labelled sentence examples. For example, using decision trees.

D. Generalise sentences and stories

i) Create a tool to help generalise and parameterise sentences, using templates and rules to create alternative sentences that mean the same thing.

ii) Use text synthesis algorithms such as GPT2 to generate plausible alternative sentences to a given example. Investigate the effectiveness of fine tuning text synthesis with metadata to produce parameterisable text
