Robotics & Manufacturing

Two of the biggest industries that are being changed by the use of IoT and deep learning are the fields of manufacturing and transportation. These fields are typically aiming for very high levels of automation and require very high accuracy as failure can lead to injury and death. They require systems that both sense and directly act on the world in a tight feedback loop. Recent advances in these fields have occurred through the use of simulations which can be used to create synthetic training data. These simulations enable the use of deep-reinforcement learning algorithms which learn by interacting with a simulation. They balance experimentation with optimisation to maximise a metric of success that may only be apparent after many actions, e.g. a self driving car reaching its destination.

Building an automated manufacturing or transportation system includes the following steps. Each step includes a list of possible projects you could focus on for this module:

A. Create affordable experimental systems

i) Develop a low latency logging and control methods for gathering experimental data from our robot arms or robotic wheelchairs and for enabling record and playback of useful behaviour.

ii) Create a low latency video streaming system using raspberry pi zeros to be mounted as sensors on the robot arm/wheel chair for teleoperation.

iii) Design and produce an image and other sensor based system to monitor the state of a CNC machine that can be used to manufacture mechanical components. Create a digital double representation to simulate the system's use and time permitting explore how to automatically validate that it is being used safely.

B. Formalise an object/situation to provide a digital double of its state

i) Develop a simulation of common electronic components that can be integrated with real-time physics systems such as PyBullet to simulate affordable robotics (e.g. a stepper motor)

ii) Adapt a real-time rigid body rag doll simulation of a person to use more realistic shape, weight and strength properties for more accurate simulations of human capabilities

iii) Create a data driven simulation of our wheelchairs movements, including realistic behaviours like wheel sliding

iv) Create a system to define the physical assembly of robotic/mechanical systems using a rigid body library to detect if there is space to move parts into place.

v) Create an electronic simulator of the electronic components and raspberry pi & arduino of our robot arms or wheelchairs. Simulate failure cases and time permitting design internal sensors to detect physical faults.

C. Gather, label and clean training data

i) Create a system to use our robot arm with our attached camera to systematically photograph objects and recreate them digitally using photogrammetry software.

ii) Generate plausible synthetic visual data for training systems (For example, scripting blender to produce photorealistic synthetic training samples of our robot arms/wheelchairs and their environment)

iii) Extract 3d models of the local area from Google Earth and automate Blender to generate plausible synthetic images that could be used by our wheel chairs to autonomously navigate. Time permitting use the data with an existing localisation algorithm or deep learning system to estimate the location of the wheel chair using visual and/or depth data.

D. Train deep learning algorithms to control mechanical systems

i) Adapt existing simulation based reinforcement learning algorithms for robotic control to the dimensions of our robotic arms or wheelchairs.
